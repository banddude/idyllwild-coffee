## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

### Business Concept

This business plan outlines the establishment of a premium independent coffee shop in Idyllwild, California, a mountain resort community serving approximately 3,000-4,000 year-round residents and tens of thousands of annual tourists. The coffee shop will differentiate itself by addressing critical market gaps: inconsistent operating hours among competitors, poor customer service, lack of comfortable gathering spaces, and absence of a reliable all-day coffee destination.

### Market Opportunity

The Idyllwild coffee market currently has 6 coffee-focused venues, each with significant limitations. Market research reveals strong demand for a coffee shop that combines quality products, consistent hours (6 AM - 7 PM, 7 days/week), exceptional hospitality, and a comfortable "third place" environment. The affluent resident base (median household income $83,205) and substantial tourist traffic from Los Angeles, San Diego, and Palm Springs create a compelling opportunity.

### Financial Highlights

**Startup Investment Required:** $200,000 - $250,000

**Projected Annual Performance (Year 3, Steady State):**
- **Annual Revenue:** $450,000 - $500,000
- **Net Profit Margin:** 15% - 18%
- **Annual Net Profit:** $67,500 - $90,000
- **Break-Even Timeline:** 14-18 months

### Competitive Advantage

1. **Consistent Operating Hours:** Only venue open 6 AM - 7 PM, 7 days/week
2. **Superior Customer Service:** Warm hospitality vs. current service complaints
3. **Third Place Environment:** Comfortable seating, WiFi, community focus
4. **Quality + Accessibility:** Locally roasted coffee with full espresso bar (addressing Black Mountain's limitations)
5. **Strategic Positioning:** Located on high-traffic downtown corridor

### Keys to Success

- Reliable, consistent operations without unexpected closures
- Exceptional customer service and community integration
- Quality coffee with transparent sourcing and fair pricing
- Comfortable environment that encourages lingering
- Effective management of seasonal cash flow fluctuations
- Strong local partnerships (bakers, suppliers, community organizations)

## 1. BUSINESS DESCRIPTION

### Business Model

Independent, locally-owned coffee shop operating as a sole proprietorship or LLC, focusing on premium coffee beverages, light food offerings, and creating a community gathering space in a mountain resort town.

### Products & Services

**Core Beverages:**
- Espresso-based drinks (lattes, cappuccinos, americanos, macchiatos)
- Drip coffee (light, medium, dark roast rotating menu)
- Pour-over specialty options
- Cold brew and iced coffee
- Seasonal specialty drinks
- Tea selection (hot and iced)
- Hot chocolate (premium, locally sourced)

**Food Offerings:**
- Partnered pastries from local bakery (Amelia's Donuts or similar)
- Breakfast items: bagels, muffins, scones
- Light lunch: sandwiches, salads (prepared off-site via commissary or partnership)
- Grab-and-go snacks
- Gluten-free and vegan options

**Additional Services:**
- Free WiFi for customers
- Community bulletin board
- Local art displays (rotating monthly)
- Private space rental for small meetings
- Coffee bean retail sales
- Potential future: beer/wine license for evening service

### Location Strategy

**Target Location:** N. Circle Drive or Village Center Drive (primary commercial corridors)

**Ideal Space:**
- **Size:** 900-1,200 square feet
- **Configuration:** 60% customer space, 40% service/back of house
- **Features:** Street-facing storefront with visibility, outdoor patio space (covered/heated), parking access

**Phase 1 (Opening, Months 1-6):**
- Monday-Sunday: 6:30 AM - 5:00 PM

**Phase 2 (Months 7-12):**
- Monday-Thursday: 6:00 AM - 6:00 PM

- Monday-Sunday: 6:00 AM - 7:00 PM

**Holiday Hours:** Open all major holidays except Thanksgiving and Christmas Day (key differentiator when competitors close)

## 2. MARKET ANALYSIS

### Target Market

**Primary Market Segments:**

**1. Year-Round Residents (40% of revenue)**
- Population: 3,000-4,000
- Demographics: Median age 50.2, median income $83,205, highly educated
- Needs: Daily coffee routine, social gathering space, remote work environment
- Frequency: 4-7 visits per week
- Average transaction: $5-$7

**2. Weekend/Seasonal Tourists (45% of revenue)**
- Volume: Tens of thousands annually
- Demographics: Middle to upper-middle class from LA/SD/Palm Springs metros
- Needs: Quality coffee before/after hikes, morning gathering spot, afternoon refreshments
- Frequency: 1-3 visits per trip (2-3 night average stays)
- Average transaction: $8-$12 (higher spending, multiple items)

**3. Special Event Visitors (10% of revenue)**
- Major events: Thanksgiving weekend, summer peak, fall foliage, PCT hiker season
- Needs: Quick service, grab-and-go options, authentic local experience
- Frequency: 1-2 visits during event
- Average transaction: $6-$10

**4. Outdoor Recreation Enthusiasts (5% of revenue)**
- Hikers, climbers, trail runners
- Needs: Early opening hours, post-activity refreshments, quick service
- Frequency: 2-4 visits per trip
- Average transaction: $5-$8

### Market Size & Growth

**Addressable Market:**
- Year-round residents: 3,000-4,000 with ~70% coffee drinkers = 2,100-2,800 potential customers
- Annual visitors: Estimated 30,000-50,000 visitor groups
- Total annual addressable market: $1.5-$2.5 million in coffee shop spending

**Growth Trends:**
- Idyllwild population growth: 7.76% (2022-2023)
- Coffee shop industry growth: 5% annually (established shops)
- Tourism recovery post-pandemic strong
- Remote work trend increasing weekday resident population

### Competitive Analysis Summary

**Direct Competitors:**
1. **Alpaca Coffee & Tea:** Modern, boba specialty, but closes at noon
2. **Pure Bean/Idyllwild Coffee Roasters:** Convenient location, longest hours, but inconsistent quality
3. **Black Mountain Coffee:** Highest quality, but very limited hours, no espresso, service issues
4. **Higher Grounds:** Longest evening hours, but customer service complaints, dated aesthetic
5. **Shala Tea Company:** Tea specialist, limited hours, not coffee-focused
6. **El Buen Cacao:** Chocolate specialist, opens late, expensive

**Competitive Advantages:**
- Most consistent hours (6 AM - 7 PM, 7 days/week)
- Full espresso bar + quality beans (combines Black Mountain quality with convenience)
- Superior customer service (addresses widespread complaints)
- Comfortable third place environment
- Transparent pricing and value proposition

**Market Entry Barriers:**
- Moderate: Market has 6 existing venues but each has significant limitations
- Capital requirements moderate ($200-250K)
- Staffing challenges in small mountain town
- Seasonal cash flow management required

## 3. ORGANIZATION & MANAGEMENT

### Ownership Structure

**Recommended:** Limited Liability Company (LLC)
- Provides liability protection
- Tax flexibility (pass-through taxation)
- Simpler than corporation for small business
- California LLC filing fee: $800 annually

### Management Team

**Phase 1 (Year 1): Owner-Operated**
- Owner/General Manager: Oversees all operations, works as primary barista/manager
- Key responsibilities: Hiring, training, vendor relationships, financials, marketing, customer service

**Phase 2 (Year 2+): Hybrid Management**
- Owner: Strategic oversight, financials, vendor relationships, marketing (30-40 hours/week)
- Assistant Manager/Shift Lead: Day-to-day operations, staff management, inventory (40 hours/week)

### Staffing Plan

**Year 1 Staffing:**
- Owner/General Manager: Full-time
- 2-3 Part-time Baristas: 20-25 hours/week each
- Total labor hours/week: 80-100 hours
- Total monthly payroll: $8,000-$10,000 (including owner draw)

**Year 2+ Staffing:**
- Owner: Part-time strategic role
- Assistant Manager/Shift Lead: Full-time (40 hours/week)
- 3-4 Part-time Baristas: 20-25 hours/week each
- Total labor hours/week: 110-140 hours
- Total monthly payroll: $12,000-$15,000

**Compensation:**
- Baristas: $17-$19/hour (above California minimum wage of $16.50)
- Shift Lead/Assistant Manager: $21-$23/hour
- Benefits: Tips pooled, employee coffee/food discount (50%), paid sick leave (California requirement)

### Advisory Support

**Professional Services:**
- **Accountant/Bookkeeper:** Monthly bookkeeping, quarterly tax prep, annual returns ($300-500/month)
- **Attorney:** Business formation, lease review, contracts (as needed, $2,000-3,000 startup)
- **Insurance Agent:** Business insurance package ($150-250/month)

**Mentors/Advisors:**
- Local Small Business Development Center (Riverside County)
- Independent coffee shop owners (outside market)
- Specialty Coffee Association resources

### Daily Operations

**Opening Procedures (5:30 AM):**
- Arrive 30 minutes before opening
- Brew drip coffee (3 roasts)
- Prepare cold brew station
- Set up pastry case (receive bakery delivery)
- Turn on espresso machine, grinders
- Count cash drawer
- Unlock doors, flip sign (6:00 AM)

**Peak Service (7:00 AM - 10:00 AM):**
- 2 staff: 1 on espresso bar, 1 on register/drip coffee
- Restock as needed
- Bus tables continuously
- Maintain clean, welcoming environment

**Mid-Day Operations (10:00 AM - 4:00 PM):**
- 1-2 staff depending on day/season
- Prep for afternoon (brew fresh coffee, restock)
- Lunch service (sandwiches, salads)
- Deeper cleaning tasks during slow periods

**Afternoon/Evening (4:00 PM - 7:00 PM):**
- 1-2 staff
- Post-hike crowd service
- Social gathering customers
- Begin closing tasks after 6:00 PM

**Closing Procedures (7:00 PM - 8:00 PM):**
- Lock doors at 7:00 PM
- Clean espresso machine, grinders
- Wipe down all surfaces
- Restock for next day
- Take out trash
- Count cash, prepare deposit
- Set alarm, lock up

### Quality Control

**Coffee Quality Standards:**
- Espresso shots pulled within 25-30 seconds
- Milk steamed to 150-160°F (not scalding)
- Drinks prepared within 3-5 minutes during rush
- Taste testing daily (espresso calibration)

**Customer Service Standards:**
- Greet every customer within 30 seconds
- Smile, make eye contact, use names when possible
- Handle complaints immediately, empower staff to comp drinks
- Ask for feedback regularly
- Respond to online reviews within 24 hours

**Cleanliness Standards:**
- Restrooms checked hourly
- Tables bused within 5 minutes
- Floors swept/mopped twice daily
- Equipment cleaned per manufacturer specs
- Health inspection prep monthly (self-audit)

## 8. RISK ANALYSIS & MITIGATION

### Market Risks

**Risk: Competition from existing venues**
- Likelihood: Medium
- Impact: Medium
- Mitigation: Strong differentiation (hours, service, environment), build loyal customer base quickly, superior marketing

**Risk: Market saturation (too many coffee shops)**
- Likelihood: Medium
- Impact: High
- Mitigation: Focus on addressing unmet needs, create true third place environment, integrate deeply with community

**Risk: Economic downturn reducing tourism**
- Likelihood: Low-Medium
- Impact: High
- Mitigation: Build strong resident base (40% of revenue), diversify revenue streams, maintain cash reserves, reduce expenses quickly if needed

### Operational Risks

**Risk: Staffing difficulties (small town labor pool)**
- Likelihood: High
- Impact: High
- Mitigation: Pay above market rates, create positive work culture, cross-train owner/manager to fill gaps, consider staff housing assistance

**Risk: Seasonal cash flow challenges**
- Likelihood: High
- Impact: Medium
- Mitigation: Maintain 3-month cash reserve, line of credit, build reserves during peak seasons, reduce variable costs during slow periods

**Risk: Weather-related closures (snow, fire, flooding)**
- Likelihood: Low-Medium
- Impact: High
- Mitigation: Business interruption insurance, emergency fund, flexible lease terms, diversify supplier locations

**Risk: Supply chain disruptions**
- Likelihood: Medium
- Impact: Medium
- Mitigation: Multiple supplier relationships, backup roaster, maintain higher inventory during peak seasons

### Financial Risks

**Risk: Construction cost overruns**
- Likelihood: Medium
- Impact: High
- Mitigation: Detailed bids with fixed pricing, 10-15% contingency budget, phased build-out if needed

**Risk: Lower than projected revenue**
- Likelihood: Medium
- Impact: High
- Mitigation: Conservative projections, owner can reduce salary temporarily, cut marketing expenses, reduce hours slightly if needed

**Risk: Equipment failure**
- Likelihood: Low-Medium
- Impact: High
- Mitigation: Equipment service contracts, backup equipment (grinders), emergency equipment rental relationships, equipment warranty coverage

### Regulatory/Legal Risks

**Risk: Health permit issues or violations**
- Likelihood: Low
- Impact: High
- Mitigation: Monthly self-audits, proper training, maintain excellent cleanliness standards, relationship with health inspector

**Risk: Changes in minimum wage or labor laws**
- Likelihood: Medium (California regularly increases)**
- Impact: Medium
- Mitigation: Build wage increases into projections (3% annually), improve efficiency, modest price increases as needed
